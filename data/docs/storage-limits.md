# Managing Storage Limits

## Checking your usage

Settings > Storage shows a breakdown of usage by file type and your total
quota. The desktop app also shows a usage bar in the tray menu.

## What happens when you're full

You can't upload new files or create new shared items once you hit your
limit. Existing files stay safe, fully accessible, and continue to sync
(read/download still works) — nothing is deleted for being over quota.

## Freeing up space

- Empty your Cloudbox trash (Settings > Trash > Empty Trash) — deleted files
  are kept for 30 days by default and still count against your quota until
  permanently removed.
- Enable "online-only files" so large files stay in the cloud and only
  download on demand, freeing local disk space (this doesn't change your
  cloud storage usage, only local disk usage).
- Remove large files you no longer need, or move them to an archive.

## Upgrading storage

Settings > Plan > Change Plan to move to a tier with more storage. See
[Plans and Pricing](billing-plans) for options. Upgrades apply immediately.

## Storage counted twice?

If usage looks higher than expected, check for duplicate files, large
version history (older versions of frequently-edited files are kept for 30
days and count toward quota), and shared folders you own, since owned
shared-folder content counts against the owner's quota, not members'.
